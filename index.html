‚ö° Developing a Lean Business Model

What is a Lean Business Model?

A Lean Business Model focuses on creating more value for customers with fewer resources. It emphasizes rapid experimentation, validated learning, and iterative product development.

Core Principles of Lean Startup

  1. Build-Measure-Learn: Create a minimum viable product, measure its performance, and learn from the results
  2. Validated Learning: Test assumptions through experiments rather than relying on intuition
  3. Pivot or Persevere: Be ready to change direction based on what you learn
  4. Minimum Viable Product (MVP): Build the simplest version that allows you to test your hypothesis

üîç Evaluating Assumptions Using Prototypes

Key Assumptions to Test

  1. Customer Problem: Does the problem really exist?
  2. Solution Fit: Does your solution solve the problem?
  3. Willingness to Pay: Will customers pay for it?
  4. Market Size: Are there enough customers?
  5. Acquisition: Can you reach customers cost-effectively?

Testing Methods

1. Customer Interviews (5-10 people)

Ask open-ended questions about their problems and observe their reactions to your prototype.

Sample Questions:

  • "Tell me about the last time you experienced [problem]"
  • "How do you currently solve this problem?"
  • "What do you think about this solution?"
  • "Would you use this? Why or why not?"

2. Landing Page Test

Create a landing page describing your product and measure interest through email signups or pre-orders.

Key Metrics: Conversion rate (target: 2-5% for cold traffic)

3. Smoke Test

Advertise your product before it's fully built to gauge demand.

Investment: $50-200 in ads

üé§ Presenting Your Business Model

The Pitch Deck Structure

A compelling pitch typically includes 10-15 slides covering:

Essential Pitch Deck Slides

  • 1. Title Slide - Company name, tagline, your name
  • 2. Problem - The pain point you're addressing
  • 3. Solution - Your product/service
  • 4. Product Demo - Show your prototype or MVP
  • 5. Market Size - TAM, SAM, SOM
  • 6. Business Model - How you make money
  • 7. Traction - Key metrics and achievements
  • 8. Competition - Competitive landscape
  • 9. Team - Key team members
  • 10. Financial Projections - 3-5 year forecast
  • 11. Ask - What you're seeking (funding, partners, etc.)

Presentation Tips

  • Tell a Story: Start with a compelling narrative that illustrates the problem
  • Use Visuals: More images, less text (Guy Kawasaki's 10/20/30 rule)
  • Practice: Rehearse until you can present without reading slides
  • Know Your Numbers: Be ready to answer questions about financials
  • Show Passion: Enthusiasm is contagious
  • Anticipate Questions: Prepare for common objections
üí° The 10/20/30 Rule:
  • 10 slides maximum
  • 20 minutes presentation time
  • 30-point minimum font size

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ‚ùå Too much text on slides
  • ‚ùå Unrealistic financial projections
  • ‚ùå Ignoring competition
  • ‚ùå Focusing on features instead of benefits
  • ‚ùå Not knowing your audience
  • ‚ùå Going over time

üé™ Business Fair Preparation

What is a Business Fair?

A business fair is an event where entrepreneurs showcase their business ideas, prototypes, or products to potential investors, partners, customers, and peers. It's an opportunity to network, get feedback, and potentially secure support for your venture.

Booth Setup Essentials

  1. Banner/Signage: Large, clear company name and tagline
  2. Prototype/Product Display: Make it interactive if possible
  3. Marketing Materials: Business cards, brochures, one-pagers
  4. Digital Presentation: Tablet or laptop with pitch deck
  5. Demonstration Area: Space to show how it works
  6. Engagement Tool: Contest, giveaway, or interactive element

Business Fair Preparation Checklist

  • Design eye-catching booth signage
  • Prepare elevator pitch (30 seconds)
  • Create business cards
  • Print marketing materials
  • Set up prototype/demo
  • Prepare FAQ sheet
  • Create email signup sheet or QR code
  • Plan booth engagement activity
  • Dress professionally
  • Bring phone chargers and backup materials
  • Practice your pitch with team
  • Create follow-up plan for contacts

Networking at the Fair

Key Networking Strategies:

  • Engage everyone who stops by - they're potential customers or partners
  • Ask open-ended questions to understand visitor needs
  • Collect contact information (email, LinkedIn)
  • Take notes on feedback received
  • Connect with other entrepreneurs - they're valuable allies
  • Follow up within 48 hours after the event
